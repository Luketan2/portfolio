About me

luke.tan04@gmail.com  ·  0408 714 149

I'm a final-year Robotics and Mechatronics Engineering student at Monash University, on the AI stream, graduating in 2026 with a High Distinction average. I'm also Co-Science Lead on Monash Nova Rover, where I help design and build the science payloads we take to international rover competitions.

I've always just liked building things, and that never really stopped as I grew up, so engineering felt like a natural place to land. What I enjoy most is the hands-on side and the problem solving, especially when a project comes with unusual or awkward constraints that don't have an obvious answer and you have to think your way to something creative.

Most of my work has to hold up in the real world rather than just on paper: dusty desert competitions, tight deadlines, and limited budgets. I'm comfortable working across a lot of different engineering areas, from fabrication, CNC machining and CAD through to coding, firmware and electricals, which means I can take on a whole system from start to finish rather than just one part of it.
